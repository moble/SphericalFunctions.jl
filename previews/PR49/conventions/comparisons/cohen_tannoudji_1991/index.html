Cohen-Tannoudji (1991)

Summary

Cohen-Tannoudji's definition of the spherical harmonics agrees with the definition used in the SphericalFunctions package.

TODO: Compare angular-momentum operators and rotation operator.

Cohen-Tannoudji et al. [29], by a Nobel-prize winner and collaborators, is an extensive two-volume set on quantum mechanics that is widely used in graduate courses.

They define spherical coordinates in the usual (physicist's) way in Chapter VI. They then compute the angular-momentum operators as [Eqs. (D-5)]

\[\begin{aligned} L_x &= i \hbar \left( \sin\phi \frac{\partial} {\partial \theta} + \frac{\cos\phi}{\tan\theta} \frac{\partial} {\partial \phi} \right), \\ L_y &= i \hbar \left( -\cos\phi \frac{\partial} {\partial \theta} + \frac{\sin\phi}{\tan\theta} \frac{\partial} {\partial \phi} \right), \\ L_z &= \frac{\hbar}{i} \frac{\partial} {\partial \phi}. \end{aligned}\]

In Complement $\mathrm{B}_{\mathrm{VI}}$ they define a rotation operator $R$ as acting on a state such that [Eq. (21)]

\[\langle \mathbf{r} | R | \psi \rangle = \langle \mathscr{R}^{-1} \mathbf{r} | \psi \rangle.\]

For an infinitesimal rotation through angle $d\alpha$ about the axis $\mathbf{u}$, he shows [Eq. (49)]

\[R_{\mathbf{u}}(d\alpha) = 1 - \frac{i}{\hbar} d\alpha \mathbf{L}.\mathbf{u}.\]
