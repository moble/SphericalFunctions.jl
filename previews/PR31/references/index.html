Another approach that was never precisely implemented in this package was due to Fukushima [11], who showed that using "X-numbers", wherein the exponent is stored as a separate integer, (implemented in this package) in the core of the recursion could increase the range to ‚Ñì‚âà2¬≥¬≤. Xing et al. showed that Fukushima's results exhibited increased error for certain angles, whereas their Eqs. The other major functionality of this package is map2salm / salm2map, which decomposes function values on regular grids into mode weights (coefficients), and vice versa. The approach used here is taken from Reinecke and Seljebotn [4], with weights based on the method by Waldvogel [7]. However, this interface has been superseded by the SSHT object, which implements several approaches, including the Reinecke-Seljebotn-Waldvogel method, as well as the optimal-dimensionality method due to Elahi et al. [5], as well as a new unpublished optimal-dimensionality method I (Mike Boyle) created.
